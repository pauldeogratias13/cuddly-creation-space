01 · Platform Vision & Core Pillars
==================================

NEXUS is engineered to outcompete every incumbent — social, streaming, commerce, and AI — in a
single platform. It is AI-first, streaming-native, privacy-preserving, and built to be unmissable.

- Intent-First Feed: Users set mood & intent (learn / create / explore / chill). Feed serves
  exclusively matching content — no algorithmic bait-and-switch.

- AI Identity Layer: Every user has an AI twin that learns their style, drafts replies,
  summarises their feed, and acts as an autonomous agent.

- Evolving Content: Posts can be forked, remixed, and evolved. Content is a living tree tracked
  like a Git repository — full provenance chain.

- Reputation Graph: Trust scores replace vanity metrics. Influence in 'design' is separate from
  'coding.' Earned, not bought.

- Micro-Communities: Auto-forming ephemeral rooms around trending topics. They appear,
  peak, and dissolve — preventing stagnation.

- Long-Form Streaming: Full-length movies, series, and live events streamed at cinema quality with
  adaptive bitrate, spatial audio, and chapter navigation.

- Watch Together: Real-time synchronized watch parties with spatial audio, live reactions,
  and AI-hosted commentary overlays.

03 · NexOS — The App Ecosystem Inside NEXUS
============================================

NexOS is NEXUS's most powerful differentiator: a platform-within-a-platform. Paying subscribers
can commission bespoke web or mobile applications built by the NEXUS engineering team and
hosted inside the NEXUS environment.

▸ How NexOS Works

1. Request & Tender: Subscribed user submits an app brief via a guided wizard: purpose, audience,
   features, branding. The NEXUS team reviews and prices the build.

2. Build & Sandbox: The NEXUS team builds the app in an isolated NexOS sandbox container. Both
   web and mobile (WebView wrapper) formats are supported.

3. App Launch Inside NEXUS: The finished app appears in the user's NexOS dashboard and in
   NEXUS's app directory — visible to relevant users based on recommendations or sponsorship.

4. Visibility Rules: Apps are shown to users based on interest graph, geographic proximity, and
   explicit subscription/follow. No spam distribution.

5. Revenue & Billing: App owner sets pricing: free, freemium, one-time purchase, or
   subscription. NEXUS takes a 20% platform fee. Payouts via Stripe.

6. Web + Mobile Parity: Every NexOS app is responsive. Web version runs in the in-app browser.
   Mobile version is a sandboxed WebView within the NEXUS app shell.

7. Update & Maintenance: App owners can request updates via the same tender flow. NEXUS team
   maintains infrastructure, security patching, and uptime SLAs.

▸ NexOS Technical Architecture

- Sandbox Runtime: Each NexOS app runs in a V8 isolate (Cloudflare Workers model) or Docker
  container with strict resource quotas (CPU, memory, egress).

- App Storage: Per-app PostgreSQL schema or object storage bucket. Isolated — no app can read
  another's data.

- Auth Bridge: NEXUS SSO: users authenticate once and access NexOS apps with their NEXUS
  identity. Apps never see raw credentials.

- API Access: NexOS apps can consume approved NEXUS APIs: user profile, social graph,
  payments, notifications. Access scoped by OAuth 2.1 consent.

- Domain & Routing: Each app gets a subdomain (appname.nexus.app) and optionally maps a
  custom domain. TLS managed by NEXUS.

- Monitoring: NexOS app health, latency, and error rates are visible to the app owner in a
  self-serve dashboard. NEXUS SRE handles P0 incidents.

NexOS turns NEXUS into a 'house with everything.' A user can have their business website, their
booking app, and their loyalty programme — all inside NEXUS, all connected to their NEXUS
identity and payments.

04 · Advertising Engine — Smart, Respectful, Profitable
======================================================

NEXUS advertising is built on a founding principle: ads must serve the user's current intent, not
interrupt it. An ad shown to someone in 'learning' mode about photography should be a camera —
not a pizza delivery coupon. This intent-matching is NEXUS's core ad differentiator.

▸ Ad Philosophy & Intent-Matching Guard

- Intent Lock: Ads are always contextually matched to the user's declared mood/intent. A user
  in 'Gaming' mode sees gaming gear; 'Shopping' mode sees product ads; 'Chill' mode sees
  lifestyle brands.

- Ad Density Control: Free tier: max 1 ad per 8 feed items. Standard tier: 1 per 15. Premium
  tier: 0 ads (ad-free). Ads never interrupt video playback mid-stream.

- Frequency Cap: No user sees the same ad more than 3x per day. Cross-surface frequency
  managed globally (feed + browse + stories counted together).

- Negative Signals: A 'Not Interested' tap suppresses that brand for 30 days. Three taps =
  permanent suppression. User trust is non-negotiable.

- Transparency Log: Users can open 'Why am I seeing this?' on any ad. Full log of signals
  used. Data usage is auditable by the user.

▸ Ad Formats

- Native Feed Cards: Sponsored content blends with organic feed. Clearly labelled 'Sponsored.'
  Same aspect ratios as organic posts.

- Stream Pre-Roll: 6-second non-skippable pre-roll OR 15-second skippable (after 5s). No
  mid-roll on content under 20 minutes.

- Commerce Spotlight: Product cards in shopping feed. Click-to-purchase in one tap.
  Commission-based pricing for advertisers.

- Site / Browse Banner: Non-intrusive banner ads in the in-app browser (top strip only). Never
  full-screen; never pop-over.

- Gaming Interstitial: Rewarded ads in the gaming hub: 'Watch 30s for 50 XP.' Opt-in only.
  Zero interstitials during active gameplay.

- Creator Sponsored Post: Brands partner with NEXUS creators. Content is co-reviewed.
  Disclosure labels auto-applied per FTC/ASA guidelines.

- NexOS App Promotion: NexOS app owners can promote their app to relevant users. CPC and CPM
  models available.

▸ Ad Targeting & Privacy Stack

- Contextual First: Primary signal is current session context (intent, content category, search
  terms) — not personal data. GDPR-safe by design.

- Interest Cohorts: Users grouped into interest cohorts (never individually profiled
  externally). Advertisers bid on cohorts, not people.

- On-Device Inference: Interest inference runs on-device via Core ML / ML Kit. Raw behaviour
  data never leaves the device.

- No Third-Party Cookies: Zero third-party tracking. No Facebook Pixel, no Google Tag Manager.
  NEXUS is a walled, privacy-first garden.

- Consent Framework: IAB TCF 2.2 compliant. First-party consent managed in NEXUS settings.
  Granular controls per ad category.

▸ Revenue Architecture

- CPM (Display): Cost-per-thousand impressions for native feed cards and browse banners.
  Floor price: €2.50 CPM (free tier), €6.00 CPM (standard tier — higher-quality audience).

- CPV (Video Pre-Roll): Cost-per-view. Charged only on 80%+ completion (non-skip) or any
  skip after 5s. Floor price: €0.04/view.

- CPC (Commerce Cards): Cost-per-click for product cards and app promotions. Floor: €0.15/click.
  Attribution window: 7-day click, 1-day view.

- Revenue Share (Creators): Creators receive 60% of ad revenue generated on their content.
  Platform retains 40%. Paid out monthly via Stripe.

- NexOS App Commission: 20% platform fee on all NexOS app revenue (subscriptions + one-time
  purchases). App owner receives 80%.

- Subscription Revenue: 100% of subscription fees retained by NEXUS minus payment processing
  (~2.9% Stripe). No revenue share on subscription tier.

07 · Video Streaming Platform — Long-Form & Cinema
=================================================

4K HDR · Dolby Atmos · Adaptive bitrate · Global CDN · Offline-first · DRM-protected

▸ Ingest & Transcoding Pipeline

- Multi-Format Ingest: Accept ProRes, MXF, MP4, MKV, and RAW camera formats. Automated scene
  detection, chapter extraction, and metadata enrichment on upload.

- AV1 Primary Codec: AV1 (libaom/SVT-AV1) for 30-50% bandwidth savings over H.264 at same
  quality. H.265/HEVC for legacy devices. HDR10, HDR10+, Dolby Vision, HLG profiles.

- Per-Title Encoding: ML-driven per-title bitrate ladders (Netflix-style). Each movie analysed
  for complexity; action scenes get higher bitrate slots automatically.

- Parallel Transcoding: FFmpeg 7 pipelines run on GPU clusters (NVIDIA A100). A 2-hour 4K
  movie transcodes to all quality variants in under 8 minutes.

- VMAF Quality Gate: Every output segment scored with VMAF. Minimum VMAF 93 enforced —
  segments that fail auto-retry at higher bitrate.

- Audio Processing: Dolby Atmos (EC-3/AC-4), multi-track audio (5.1, 7.1, stereo), audio
  normalisation to -14 LUFS, and automatic loudness metering per EBU R128.

- Subtitle & Caption: Auto-generated SRT/WebVTT via Whisper Large v3. Forced narrative
  captions embedded as separate tracks. SDH and closed captions to FCC/CVAA compliance.

▸ DRM & Content Protection

- Multi-DRM Stack: Widevine (Android/Chrome) · FairPlay (Apple) · PlayReady (Windows/Xbox).
  Single CENC encryption, multiple license servers.

- Hardware Security Level: Widevine L1 (TEE-backed) required for 4K/HDR playback. L3 allowed
  for 1080p. Hardware DRM coverage: 85%+ of active devices.

- Offline Downloads: Secure offline download with license-bound decryption. Expiry enforced at
  client. Downloaded content encrypted with device-bound key.

- Watermarking: Forensic watermarking embeds invisible user identifier in video stream. Leak
  source traceable to specific account within 30 seconds of clip.

08 · Backend Services
=====================

Microservices. gRPC-native. Failure-tolerant. Event-sourced.

▸ User & Identity Service — Go

- Passkeys (WebAuthn): Passwordless authentication. FIDO2 hardware-bound keys. Phishing-proof
  by design.

- Decentralised Identity: Optional DID (Decentralised Identifiers) for self-sovereign identity
  — portable across platforms.

- Zero-Knowledge Proofs: Age verification and credential checks without revealing underlying
  data (zk-SNARKs via gnark).

- Multi-Persona Vault: Cryptographically isolated personas. Switching identity generates a new
  ephemeral session token.

- Adaptive MFA: Risk-scored MFA: low-risk logins skip 2FA, high-risk enforce hardware key.

▸ Feed Ranking Engine — Rust

- Two-Tower Neural Retrieval: Candidate retrieval via approximate nearest-neighbour on 512-dim
  user + content embeddings. Sub-5ms retrieval at 1B items.

- Multi-Armed Bandit Re-Ranker: Thompson Sampling balances exploit (known preferences) vs
  explore (serendipity). Boredom detected in <10 interactions.

- Real-Time Feature Pipeline: User signals (dwell time, replays, skips) update embeddings in
  real time via Kafka → Flink → feature store.

- WASM Plugin System: Third-party ranking plugins compiled to WebAssembly — safe sandboxed
  execution inside the ranker with microsecond overhead.

  Redis Stack adds search + JSON + TimeSeries.

- Qdrant: Vector similarity search for content recommendations, semantic search, and
  in-video scene retrieval.

- Neo4j AuraDB: Social graph traversal. Cypher queries for friend-of-friend, interest
  clustering, and trust propagation.

12 · Security Architecture
===========================

Zero-trust. E2E encrypted. SLSA Level 3. DRM-hardened.

- Zero-Trust Network: No implicit trust. Every request authenticated and authorised. mTLS
  between all services.

- Supply Chain Security: SLSA Level 3. SBOM for every build. Cosign signs all container images.
  Dependabot + Renovate.

- Secret Management: HashiCorp Vault with dynamic secrets. DB credentials rotate every 15
  minutes. No long-lived credentials in env vars.

- E2E Encrypted Chat: Signal Protocol (Double Ratchet + X3DH). Server is blind to message
  content. Keys never leave devices.

- DRM Security: Widevine L1 TEE for 4K. Forensic watermarking (user-ID embedded). Screen
  capture API blocking.

- DDoS / Bot Defence: Cloudflare Turnstile (invisible CAPTCHA) · ML-based bot fingerprinting ·
  Token bucket rate limiting.

- Ad Fraud Prevention: ML-based click fraud detection. Invalid Traffic (IVT) scoring per
  impression. Advertisers credited for fraudulent impressions.

13 · Engineering Principles
============================

- Default to Open Standards: Prefer IETF/W3C/CNCF over proprietary lock-in. Every vendor
  choice must have an open alternative.

- Design for Failure: Every service assumes dependencies will fail. Circuit breakers, retries
  with exponential backoff, and fallback paths are mandatory.

- Data Minimisation: Collect only what is needed. Retention policies enforced at storage
  layer, not just in policy documents.

- Progressive Delivery: Feature flags for 100% of releases. Dark launches, canary at
  1% → 10% → 50% → GA. Rollback in <60 seconds.

- Async Everything: Synchronous calls only for user-facing critical path. Everything else:
  message queue, event stream, or background job.

- Measure Everything: SLIs for every external API. Error budget burn rate alerts. Chaos tests
  in staging weekly. Video QoE tracked per CDN PoP.

- Streaming-Native Design: Video is a first-class primitive, not an afterthought. QoE metrics
  gate every deploy.

- Intent Respect Principle: No feature, ad, or notification may interrupt a user who has
  declared their current intent. Mood context gates all push surfaces.

Build the platform users can't imagine living without.
